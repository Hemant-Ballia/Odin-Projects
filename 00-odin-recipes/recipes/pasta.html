Pasta

Description

Pasta is a staple of Italian cuisine, made primarily from durum wheat flour mixed with water or eggs. It comes in many shapes and sizes, each suited to different types of sauces and cooking methods. Pasta can be served hot or cold and is enjoyed worldwide for its versatility, simplicity, and comforting taste.

Popular types of pasta include spaghetti, penne, fusilli, macaroni, and lasagna sheets, among many others. It pairs well with a variety of sauces—from simple tomato-based sauces and creamy Alfredo to rich meat ragùs and fresh pesto. Pasta dishes are often garnished with cheese, herbs, and vegetables to create flavorful meals for any occasion.

Ingredients:

  • 12 ounces pasta (spaghetti, penne, fusilli, or your choice)
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1 cup fresh spinach or basil leaves
  • ½ cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Optional: red pepper flakes for heat

Steps:

  1. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Cook pasta according to package directions until al dente. Drain and set aside.
  2. In a large skillet, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add minced garlic and sauté until fragrant, about 1-2 minutes.
  3. Add cherry tomatoes to the skillet and cook until they start to soften, about 4-5 minutes.
  4. Stir in fresh spinach or basil and cook until wilted, about 1-2 minutes.
  5. Add the cooked pasta to the skillet and toss to combine with the vegetables and garlic.
  6. Season with salt, pepper, and red pepper flakes (if using). Stir in grated Parmesan cheese.
  7. Serve immediately, garnished with extra Parmesan or fresh herbs if desired.
