Lasagna

Description

Lasagna is a classic Italian dish made by layering flat pasta sheets with rich meat or vegetable sauce, creamy cheese mixtures, and often topped with melted cheese. It is baked until bubbly and golden, resulting in a hearty and comforting meal loved worldwide.

This dish is celebrated for its versatility, allowing endless variations including vegetarian and gluten-free options. Whether served as a family dinner or at festive gatherings, lasagna offers warmth and satisfaction with every bite, blending flavors and textures that delight the senses.

Ingredients:

  • pasta
  • Tomato sauce
  • Cheese

Steps:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. Cook 12 lasagna noodles in salted boiling water until al dente. Drain and rinse with cold water; set aside.
  3. In a large skillet, brown ½ pound lean ground beef, ½ pound Italian sausage, 1 diced yellow onion, and 2 minced garlic cloves over medium-high heat until fully cooked. Drain excess fat.
  4. Stir in 36 ounces of pasta sauce, 2 tablespoons tomato paste, 1 teaspoon Italian seasoning, salt, and pepper. Simmer for 5 minutes.
  5. In a bowl, mix 2 cups ricotta cheese (or cottage cheese), 1½ cups shredded mozzarella cheese, ¼ cup shredded Parmesan cheese, ¼ cup chopped fresh parsley, 1 beaten egg, salt, and pepper.
  6. In a 9x13 inch baking dish, spread about 1 cup of the meat sauce on the bottom.
  7. Layer 3 cooked lasagna noodles over the sauce.
  8. Spread one-third of the cheese mixture over the noodles.
  9. Spoon 1 cup of meat sauce over the cheese layer.
  10. Repeat layering two more times: noodles, cheese mixture, and meat sauce.
  11. Top with a final layer of noodles and the remaining meat sauce.
  12. Cover the dish with aluminum foil and bake for 45 minutes.
  13. Remove the foil and sprinkle the remaining mozzarella and Parmesan cheese on top.
  14. Bake uncovered for an additional 15 minutes until golden and bubbly.
  15. Let the lasagna rest for 15 minutes before cutting and serving.
